Upload Cadence Doesn't Matter as Much as You Think

March 3, 2026

📅"Post consistently" is the most common YouTube advice. It's also the most misleading. As Ali Abdaal's YouTube producer, I've analyzed upload patterns across dozens of channels. What I've found is that cadence matters far less than most creators think.

Analysis as of March 2026 · Based on 776 long-form YouTube videos across 57 channels.

Every YouTube guru will tell you the growth formula: "Post 3 times a week, be consistent, the algorithm rewards consistency." Sounds logical, right? It's mostly wrong.

Does posting frequency matter on YouTube?

Less than you'd imagine. When I audit channels using the Channel Audit tool, I track uploads weekly and monthly. What I consistently see is this: channels posting once a week with strong packaging often outperform those posting daily with mediocre thumbnails and titles.

The algorithm doesn't directly reward frequency. It rewards viewer satisfaction, full stop. That's measured by click-through rate, watch time, and engagement. One video with a 70% average view duration and 10% CTR will get pushed harder than three videos with 30% AVD and 3% CTR.

What actually matters more than upload frequency

  1. Packaging quality. A channel that posts once a week with A+ thumbnails and titles will beat one posting daily with C-grade packaging. Every single time. I've seen this pattern in every audit I've ever done.
  2. Topic selection. Are you even making videos people want to watch? A well-chosen topic with modest production will always beat a beautifully produced video about something nobody cares about.
  3. First 30 seconds. This is your hook. If you lose viewers early, it doesn't matter how long the video is or how often you post. The algorithm catches that early drop-off and just stops recommending the video.
  4. Average view duration. YouTube's single most important metric. A 20-minute video with 60% retention (12 minutes watched) sends a far stronger signal than a 30-minute video with 30% retention (9 minutes watched), even if the second one is longer.

When does upload cadence matter?

Okay, so cadence does matter in two specific scenarios:

  • Brand new channels. When you're just starting out, posting more gives the algorithm more data points to figure out who your audience is. This is about signal volume, though, not consistency for consistency's sake.
  • Audience expectation. If your audience expects a video every Tuesday and you vanish for a month, subscribers will disengage. The fix here is communication, not frantic uploading. A quick community post saying "new video next week" does more than a rushed video this week.

The real advice

Post at whatever cadence allows you to maintain high quality. For Ali Abdaal's channel, that usually means 1–2 videos a week. If you're a solo creator without a team, once a week or even twice a month is totally fine — as long as every video hits these marks:

  • A thumbnail someone would actually click in a crowded feed.
  • A title that sparks curiosity or promises a clear outcome.
  • A hook that keeps viewers watching past the first 30 seconds.
  • Content that actually delivers on the title's promise.

If you can manage all that three times a week, fantastic. If you can only do it once a month, that's still miles better than three mediocre videos every week.

Check your upload patterns
