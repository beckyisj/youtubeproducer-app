Shorts vs Long-Form: What the Data Says

March 3, 2026

📱Should you post YouTube Shorts? It's a question I get a lot. As Ali Abdaal's YouTube producer, I'm watching both formats closely. What I've found is Shorts can definitely help with growth, but they don't often bring in the same revenue or deep audience connection as long-form.

Analysis as of March 2026 · Based on 776 long-form YouTube videos across 57 channels.

The Shorts debate has been going strong since 2021. Some creators insist they've supercharged their subscriber count. Others are convinced Shorts viewers never stick around for long-form content.

After looking at dozens of channels, I've noticed the reality sits somewhere in the middle.

Do YouTube Shorts help or hurt your channel?

Neither, on their own. They just serve fundamentally different purposes than long-form.

And confusing those two is where creators often run into trouble.

When I audit channels using both formats, the split is usually pretty dramatic. A channel might pull 500K views on long-form and 2M on Shorts.

But those Shorts viewers? Totally different engagement pattern. They're scrolling, not actively choosing to watch. The algorithmic surface is just built differently (trust me on this one).

How to compare Shorts vs long-form performance

You just can't compare raw view counts. A Short with a million views and a 15-minute video with 200K? Not even playing the same game.

What I look at instead:

  1. Watch time per view. A 15-minute video watched 60% of the way through? That's 9 minutes of watch time per viewer. A 30-second Short watched completely? Just 0.5 minutes. The long-form video is pulling in 18x more watch time per view.
  2. Subscriber conversion rate. How many viewers actually subscribe? Long-form usually converts at 2-5x the rate of Shorts. Makes sense — they've spent enough time with you to decide if they like your vibe.
  3. Revenue per view. YouTube pays way more for long-form ad views than anything from the Shorts Fund. I've seen a 100K long-form video earn more than a Short with 5 million views (seriously).

When Shorts make sense

  • New channels trying to build initial audience. Shorts can put your face in front of millions way faster than long-form. Think of them as a discovery tool, not your main content strategy.
  • Repurposing long-form clips. Grab the most compelling 30-60 seconds from your long-form video and post it. It's basically free marketing for the full thing.
  • Testing topics before committing. A Short takes minutes to put together. If a topic hits in a Short, that's a good sign it's worth a full video.

When to skip Shorts

  • If they're cannibalizing your long-form production time. One really good long-form video a week beats five Shorts and a mediocre long-form video, every time.
  • If your Shorts audience doesn't overlap with long-form. Go check your analytics. If those Shorts viewers aren't sticking around for your long-form content, you're essentially building two separate audiences. That's double the work for potentially half the impact.
  • If you're in a niche where depth matters. Some topics just can't be squeezed into 60 seconds without losing their value. Think finance education, deep-dive analysis, interview content — those are long-form by nature.
